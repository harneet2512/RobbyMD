=== SCRIPT: fatigue and unintentional weight loss ===

DIALOGUE (60–90 seconds when read aloud, ~150–250 words):
DR: Good morning, Mr. Smith. What brings you in?
PT: I've been really tired for months now. And I think I'm losing weight without trying.
DR: How much weight, and over what period?
PT: Maybe fifteen pounds over the last three months. My clothes are loose.
DR: Any change in appetite?
PT: Actually, my appetite is okay — sometimes I feel hungrier than usual, but the weight keeps coming off.
DR: Any night sweats or fevers?
PT: I wake up damp sometimes, yeah. Not every night.
DR: How's your mood been? Sleep?
PT: Sleep is okay. Mood, I guess I've been down, but I think it's because I feel so tired all the time.
DR: You said fifteen pounds — are you sure about that number?
PT: Let me think. It might be closer to twenty. I was 185, and I weighed myself this morning at 165.
DR: Any bowel changes, blood in stool, or urinary changes?
PT: I go to the bathroom more than I used to. I'm thirsty all the time.
DR: Any lumps, or a history of cancer in the family?
PT: No lumps I've noticed. My mother had breast cancer at sixty.
DR: Any medications?
PT: I take metformin for pre-diabetes, and levothyroxine for my thyroid.
DR: I'd like to get some labs — a CBC, TSH, metabolic panel, and hemoglobin A1c.
