=== SCRIPT: dizziness and syncope ===

DIALOGUE (60–90 seconds when read aloud, ~150–250 words):
DR: Hello, Ms. Smith. Tell me what happened.
PT: I passed out this morning. I was standing in the kitchen and the next thing I knew I was on the floor.
DR: Any warning before you passed out?
PT: I felt lightheaded, a bit sweaty, and my vision went gray around the edges.
DR: How long were you out?
PT: My husband said maybe ten seconds. I came to quickly.
DR: Any chest pain, palpitations, or shortness of breath before it happened?
PT: No chest pain. My heart felt like it was racing for a second, but it was quick.
DR: Did you bite your tongue or lose bladder control?
PT: No, nothing like that.
DR: You said your heart was racing — was that before or after you felt lightheaded?
PT: Actually, thinking about it, the racing came first. Then the lightheaded feeling, then I went down.
DR: Okay, that's important. Any similar episodes before?
PT: I've felt dizzy when I stand up quickly, but I've never passed out before.
DR: Any recent illness, fluid loss, or changes in medication?
PT: I had a stomach bug two days ago, some diarrhea. I haven't been drinking much water.
DR: What medications do you take?
PT: I'm on metoprolol and hydrochlorothiazide for blood pressure. And a baby aspirin.
DR: I'd like to check orthostatic vitals and get an EKG.
