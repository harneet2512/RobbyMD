=== SCRIPT: headache ===

DIALOGUE (60–90 seconds when read aloud, ~150–250 words):
DR: Hi, Ms. Smith. What brings you in today?
PT: I've got a really bad headache. I've never had one like this.
DR: When did it start?
PT: About two hours ago. It hit me all at once, like something exploded in my head.
DR: Where is the pain?
PT: All over, but mostly the back of my head and my neck feels stiff.
DR: On a scale of one to ten?
PT: A ten. It's the worst headache of my life.
DR: Any nausea, vomiting, or vision changes?
PT: I feel sick to my stomach, and bright light really bothers me.
DR: Any weakness, numbness, or trouble speaking?
PT: No, nothing like that.
DR: You said this started two hours ago — was it sudden or gradual?
PT: Actually, it's been closer to an hour. I was trying to wait it out before coming in.
DR: Okay. Any history of migraines?
PT: I get headaches sometimes, but they're never like this. This is different.
DR: Any medications?
PT: I took two acetaminophen about thirty minutes ago. No relief. I'm also on sumatriptan for my usual migraines, but I haven't taken it today.
DR: Any family history of aneurysm or stroke?
PT: My aunt had a brain bleed in her fifties.
DR: I'm going to examine your neck and do a neurologic exam now.
