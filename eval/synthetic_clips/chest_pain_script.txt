=== SCRIPT: chest pain ===

DIALOGUE (60–90 seconds when read aloud, ~150–250 words):
DR: Good morning, Mr. Johnson. What brings you in today?
PT: I've been having this pressure in my chest. It started about two hours ago.
DR: Can you describe what it feels like?
PT: Like something heavy sitting on my chest. Dull, not sharp.
DR: Does it radiate anywhere — your arm, jaw, back?
PT: A little into my left arm, and up to my jaw.
DR: On a scale of one to ten, how bad is it right now?
PT: Around a six. It was worse when I was walking to the car.
DR: Anything make it better or worse?
PT: It got better when I sat down. Worse with walking.
DR: Any shortness of breath, sweating, or nausea?
PT: I felt sweaty at first. A little short of breath too.
DR: You said it started two hours ago — was this the first episode, or have you felt it before?
PT: Actually, now that I think about it, I had something similar yesterday evening after dinner. It went away in a few minutes so I didn't mention it.
DR: Got it. Any history of heart disease, high blood pressure, or diabetes?
PT: High blood pressure for about ten years. My father had a heart attack at fifty-eight.
DR: What medications are you on?
PT: Amlodipine for my blood pressure, atorvastatin for cholesterol, and a daily aspirin.
DR: Any smoking or alcohol?
PT: I smoked a pack a day for thirty years, quit five years ago.
DR: I want to get an ECG and a troponin, and apply the HEART score to assess your risk.
