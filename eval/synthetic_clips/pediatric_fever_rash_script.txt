=== SCRIPT: pediatric fever rash ===

DIALOGUE (60–90 seconds when read aloud, ~200–240 words):
DR: Hi, I'm Doctor Patel. What's going on with Emma today?
PT: She's had a fever for five days now. It keeps going up and down. The highest was a hundred and three last night.
DR: Any other symptoms along with the fever?
PT: She has this rash. It started on her trunk and now it's spread to her arms too. And her lips look really red and cracked.
DR: When did the rash first show up?
PT: Thursday, I think. Actually wait, no, it was Friday — Thursday she just had the fever, the rash showed up Friday morning.
DR: Okay, Friday then. How old is Emma now, and what does she weigh?
PT: She's fourteen months, and she was about ten kilos at her last check.
DR: Any trouble with feeding, sleep, or her usual behavior?
PT: She's been really cranky, won't eat much, and mostly just wants to sleep.
DR: What have you been giving her for the fever?
PT: Tylenol. The infant one, a hundred and sixty milligrams per five milliliters. I've been giving her about three milliliters every six hours.
DR: Let me come back to that dose in a minute — at ten kilos she's probably underdosed. Is she up to date on her vaccinations?
PT: Yes, everything except the MMR. That one's scheduled for next month.
DR: Let me take a look at her mouth. I can see those strawberry tongue bumps and the cracked lips you described. With five days of fever, rash on her trunk and extremities, and these oral changes, I want to work her up for Kawasaki disease today.
